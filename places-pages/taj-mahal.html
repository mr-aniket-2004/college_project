Taj Mahal , Agra

Overview

Majestically located on the banks of Yamuna River, this wonder of the world was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan as a memorial for his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Suggested Duration For Exploring :

Around 5 Hours

What To Expect?

Built in ivory-white marble, this mausoleum flaunts Indo-Islamic architecture. Tourists can walk through the manicured gardens dotted with fountains, and capture beautiful sunset shots.

Tips :

  • If visiting for the first time, it is better to hire a guide to learn about the history of the monument.li>
  • Wear colourful attire for amazing photographs against the all-white background.
  • Visit during the early morning hours to avoid the crowds.
  • Best to visit the monument between November and February.

Description :

Taj Mahal, located in Agra in the state of Uttar Pradesh, is widely considered as a symbol of eternal love. It is a monument built in the 17th Century. It was built during the Mughal rule, and can be summed up as a brilliant representation of grandeur that Mughal architecture stood for. Built under the reign of Shah Jahan, the Mughal emperor, Taj Mahal is actually a mausoleum of his beloved and consort Mumtaj Mahal. Its ethereal beauty typically signifies the beauty of his beloved, in whose memory this architectural genius was created. Taj Mahal is also one of the Seven Wonders of the world, popularly known for its white marble construction and surrounding greenscape. It also earns a place among UNESCO's World Heritage Sites.

Best time to Visit Taj Mahal :

Located at a distance of 200 km from the national capital New Delhi, the Taj Mahal in Agra is easily accessible through roadways and railways. Buses and cars take the Yamuna Expressway to reach here. If arriving by train, book a ticket to the Agra Railway Station and hire a cab or taxi from there to reach the Taj Mahal.

Times for visitors :

  • Opens: 30 minutes before sunrise
  • Closes: 30 minutes before sunset
  • Closed on: Fridays
  • Night view timings: 8.30 pm to 12.30 pm (Visiting in 8 batches with maximum 50 people each batch)

Entry Fess :

  • Indians: INR 50 for entry + INR 200 for main mausoleum
  • Citizens of SAARC and BIMSTEC countries: INR 540 entry + INR 200 for main mausoleum
  • Foreigner tourists: INR 1100 entry + INR 200 for main mausoleum
  • Children under 15 years of age: Free entry
