Heritage Sites In India

Explore the list of best Heritage destinations in India

Taj Mahal, Agra

Majestically located on the banks of Yamuna River, this wonder of the world was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan as a memorial for his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is one of the wonder in world

EXPLORE

Qutub Minar, Delhi

Towering 73 metres high above the greens in Mehrauli, construction for this five-storey minaret was started by Qutb ud Din Aibak, founder of Delhi Sultanate. It is renowned for the artistic brick-work on its pillars.

EXPLORE

Red Fort, Delhi

Located in Chandni Chowk Old Delhi, this red sandstone structure was constructed under the reign of Mughal emperor Shah Jahan for protection against invaders. It is known for its intricate Mughal architecture.

EXPLORE

Ellora Caves, Aurangabad

Featuring Buddhist, Hindu and Jain monuments, Ellora Caves are a stunning rock-cut temple complex with 34 monasteries and temples. It takes about an hour by road to reach Ellora from Aurangabad.

EXPLORE

Hampi, Karnataka

The renowned UNESCO World Heritage Site and the once-magnificent capital of the Vijayanagara Empire; Hampi attracts backpackers and pilgrims in equal numbers due to its intriguing ruins of forts and temples.
